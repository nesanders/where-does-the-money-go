Welcome to the Campaign Finance Analyzer for Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez (AOC). Data presented here is sourced directly from the Federal Election Commission (FEC) processed data for the Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez for Congress committee. Every transaction above a certain threshold is publicly reported under strict transparency rules.

Is AOC's campaign typical? No, her campaign finance structure is highly atypical for a federal candidate. While many politicians rely primarily on corporate Political Action Committees (PACs) and high-dollar fundraising galas—often funneling money exclusively to high-end media consulting firms—AOC's campaign is driven predominantly by grassroots, small-dollar donors. This means her spending is heavily biased toward digital organizing, small merchandise fulfillment, and an unusually robust payroll dedicated to internal organizing staff rather than entirely outsourcing to agencies.

FEC Official Spending by Category

When campaigns file expense reports with the FEC, they must classify their disbursements (spending) into broad categories, such as "Administrative", "Fundraising", or "Travel". This high-level distribution provides an initial glance at a campaign's priorities. AOC's heavy emphasis on digital organizing, merchandise, and maintaining a well-paid staff causes certain categories to swell larger than average.

Sane Categorization (Smart Parsing)

Since raw FEC categories (like the massive "OTHER" bucket) can obscure the true functional purpose of spending, we implemented a Smart Parsing Classifier. By analyzing individual transaction memos and vendor names (e.g. mapping "Gusto" to Payroll, or "FB/Google" to Media Ads), we recalculate the budget into human-readable buckets. The charts below display this corrected, "sane" view of the campaign's finances.

Sane Categories Over Time

Tracking the functional "smart" categories over the cycle reveals precisely when hiring happens, when ad buys spike, and when SaaS/platform fees adjust.

Decoding the "OTHER" Category

A massive portion of spending falls into an opaque FEC category labeled simply as "OTHER". This happens because FEC classifications are somewhat aged and rigid, often failing to neatly categorize modern digital services. By cracking open this bucket and looking at memo descriptions and specific payees, we reveal the true operational costs: digital ad networks, high-volume credit card merchant fees, extensive payroll taxes, and SaaS subscriptions. Understanding "OTHER" is critical to understanding any modern digital campaign.

Top 10 Payees Overall

Look behind any major candidate, and you will find an ecosystem of specialized vendors. AOC's top vendor list underscores her atypical organizing strategy.

  • Middle Seat / Middle Seat Consulting: A progressive digital agency specializing in grassroots fundraising, email programs, and digital advertising.
  • BREX: A corporate credit card/expense management platform. Campaigns use Brex to distribute cards to staff to handle fast-moving operational costs, travel, and recurring software subscriptions.
  • Shopify: E-commerce platform. AOC's campaign uniquely operates a massive merchandising wing, driving fundraising and brand presence.
  • ActBlue: The ubiquitous Democratic technical platform for securely processing millions of small-dollar donations.
  • Gusto: A modern cloud-based payroll, benefits, and human resource management software utilized to pay campaign staff.
